SOlution Scenarios
Solution scenarios get people to act out possible solutions as a way of making brainstorming less strict and serious. This also helps the team see how solutions they propose might affect different parts of the organization, allowing them to modify and build on the ideas out in the open.

Case Study
Steps
1
Open by splitting up into smaller groups and sharing a prompt for a challenge you’d like to tackle from a previous issue generation activity.
Hint: Doing an example with a different challenge prompt will help break the ice and show people the potential effectiveness of the method.
2
Give each group some time to think of possible solutions, and a plan to act them out. In the first round, put an emphasis on just getting and idea out quickly, so that people get used to the method.
3
Share each team’s skits and have a discussion after each of them to build on top of the ideas presented. Think about how seeing a demonstration of the ideas affects how you feel about actually implementing them.
4
Now that each team has an idea of how to go through the activity, have another round of coming up with ideas and skits, but this time give a little more time to think of thorough ideas.
5
Present the skits again and discuss as a team about which ideas throughout the session they resonated with the most. You can take these and potentially move forward with parts of each to help solve your initial prompt.
