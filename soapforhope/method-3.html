Map Out Feelings
Often you don’t realize it but the experience of your teams and partners changes over the course of your term. This activity is a quick and lightweight way to identify moments of change, whether positive or negative, and you can use this information as a starting point to understand the reasons behind this change. This is also a great tool to show your participants that you care about their experience throughout the term.

Case Study
Steps
1
Figure out a prompt based on what you’d like to find out about your participants. Make sure this is focussed and not too long. You’ll have time later to probe on the specifics.
Hint: Think about the time periods you fill out on the activity sheet from the downloadable materials. These do not have to be separated by equal amounts of time. They have even be identified as significant moments, such as when a new policy was implemented or a new set of members were hired.
2
Find time to get each individual participant to fill out the sheet. We’ve found that while electronic mediums offer more convenience, a physical card gets participants to slow down and give more thought through responses.
3
Once all participants have filled out the card, take a look and figure out questions that you have based on the data. You can ask participants individually, or use another activity to tackle these. Remember, any feedback is valuable and you should not take any negative responses personally.
