Staff Your Team
This is a more specific activity focussed on understanding the needs of the organization as it matures over time, and visually brainstorming solutions to meet changing needs. Participants create and organize role cards for possible new structures that the organization could adapt, whether that means changing the number of people on the team, modifying responsibilities, or even creating completely new roles.

Case Study
Steps
1
Hand out role cards to either individuals or small groups and ask them to take some time to create cards for each of the roles in the organization, and then organize them on a table to show the hierarchy among roles.
Hint: Adding constraints such as staffing the team with a maximum of 7 roles, only focussing on a specific section of the organization, or asking them to make up at least one whacky role, are all ways of helping teams stay away from simply recreating the current hierarchy during the activity.
2
You’ll find that because of the tangible nature of the cards, people will start moving around and modifying their solutions as you probe them with questions. This is a good sign, so allow things to turn into a discussion and invite other teams to modify each other’s solutions.
3
You’ll want to take notes during the session, and at the end you can talk about some of the main themes or unique ideas that came up to get a final perspective on them. You can take these into consideration when the time comes to restructure the actual team.
