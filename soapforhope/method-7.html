Role Play
Being outside a certain role allows people to think about them from a fresh perspective. Unfortunately, team members often feel like it is not their place to bring up certain issues or solutions. Role playing is the perfect way to completely get rid of this notion. These roles do not even have to be real. Want to hear about how you can make the organization more fun? Assign someone to be the ”VP of Good Times” and start to get the creative juices flowing.
Download Materials
Case Study
Steps
1
Use the downloadable materials to create role cards. These do not have to be current roles in the organization, or even a conventional role for that matter.
2
Hand out cards to people to get them thinking outside their actual roles and then start a discussion on some topics you would like to address or get a fresh perspective on.
Hint: To help people feel at ease, give yourself a silly role as well, and start off to get the ball rolling.
3
Probe the discussions to generate ideas, but don’t force anyone to get too specific. The idea here is to generate high-level ideas.
4
Note some of the main ideas that come out of the discussion, and see if you can craft them into actionable solutions after the workshop is over.
