Conversation Starters
Conversation starters are a lightweight tool used to dive deeper into issues or hard-to-approach topics you’ve noticed in your team. Fun cards that are open to interpretation are used to spark discussions, without you having to dictate a conversation. You’ll be surprised by how your team interprets things and may see things in a completely different light by the end of the activity.

Case Study
Steps
1
Design your conversation starter cards around some focussed issues you want to spark debate about. Keep the cards abstract enough so it doesn’t feel like you are dictating a conversation, but focussed enough so that your team doesn’t feel lost.
Hint: The downloadable materials offer a guide on how to make good conversation starters, regardless of your artistic ability!
2
Get someone to pick a conversation starter and then ask people how they interpret that card. Focus on the discussions that come out of these, and be careful not to stop a discussion just because it’s not what you intended to come out of that specific card.
3
Once you find that people have had their voices heard about a topic, move on and let someone pick a new card.
Hint: These cards are meant to be lightweight and disposable. So if a conversation dies down early from a card, feel free to just move on to the next one without any worries. Over time, you’ll get better at making cards that get your specific team talking.
